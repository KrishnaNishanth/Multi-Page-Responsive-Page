Experiential Learning: This methodology emphasizes learning through direct experience, reflection, and application. Individuals actively participate in real world or simulated situations to acquire skills and then reflect on their experiences to gain insights and refine their abilities. Experiential learning often involves hands-on activities, case studies, simulations, and project-based learning.

Self-directed Learning: Self-directed learning empowers individuals to take responsibility for their own learning process. Learners identify their learning goals, determine the resources and strategies they will use, and engage in independent study and practice. Self-directed learning often involves setting goals, creating learning plans, conducting research, and seeking feedback to monitor progress.

Active Learning: Active learning promotes engagement and participation by encouraging learners to be actively involved in the learning process. It focuses on problem-solving, discussions, group activities, and practical exercises. Active learning methodologies can include collaborative projects, debates, role-playing, brainstorming sessions, and interactive exercises.
